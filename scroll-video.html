Section 1: Getting Started

This page demonstrates smooth scroll-controlled video playback. As you scroll through the page, the video's playback position is controlled by your scroll movement.

Scroll down to advance the video forward, and scroll up to rewind it. The page continues to scroll normally while the video playback is synchronized with your scroll input.

Section 2: How It Works

The implementation uses the Intersection Observer API and requestAnimationFrame for smooth, performant video frame updates. Each pixel of scroll translates to a proportional change in video playback time.

The video time is clamped between 0 and the total duration, ensuring smooth playback without jumping or unexpected behavior.

Section 3: Advanced Features

The progress bar at the bottom of the video updates in real-time to show your position within the video. The time display in the top-right corner shows both current playback time and total duration.

The scroll direction indicator appears in the top-right of the screen to show whether you're scrolling up or down.

Section 4: Performance Optimized

This implementation is highly optimized for performance using throttled scroll events and requestAnimationFrame for smooth updates. It works seamlessly across desktop and mobile browsers.

The video is set to muted and playsinline for better mobile compatibility.

Section 5: Customization

You can easily customize the sensitivity of the scroll-to-video mapping by adjusting the SCROLL_SENSITIVITY constant in the JavaScript. A higher value makes the video advance faster with each scroll.

Replace the video source with your own MP4 file to use this with custom videos.

Section 6: Browser Support

This solution works on all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. Mobile browsers are fully supported with optimized touch scrolling.

The implementation uses standard HTML5 video and vanilla JavaScript with no external dependencies.
